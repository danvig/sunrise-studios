Tony Slate, Woodshop

The story of Mr. Slate is one of the more inspirational
on the Sunrise backlot. Tony lost his hearing, as well
as three fingers, working as a woodshop teacher in
several Los Angeles public high schools. Without his
fingers, sign language was a near impossibility, but
that did not stop the determined teacher from landing
a carpenter job on the set of Don't Go Downstairs.
From that point on, the sets and scenery for many of
Sunrise's best known films came from Tony Slate's
woodshop, located behind Stage 7 under the shadow
of the Sunrise parking garage. Because of our
constant production schedule, Tony's shop is always
buzzing with the sounds of skill saws, jigsaws,
sanders, and drills.
