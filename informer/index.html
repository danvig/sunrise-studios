Saturday, March 4, 2000

RIFKIN WRAPPED, ROMAN RULES

ollywood horror legend Fred Rifkin is out, hot
young music vid helmer Roman Bridger is in as
director of the much anticipated Stab 3. The
decision, made by producer John Milton yesterday, marks
the end of a rocky first two weeks of preproduction.
Citing "creative differences," Milton told reporters that he
and Fred "were not seeing the same picture." Milton had
"no choice" but to replace the aging vet with "somebody
young, hip and fresh, somebody like Roman Bridger."
Bridger recently picked up an MTV Video Music Award
for his psychedelic beach party video "Hoochie Chase" by
the Booty Bruthas.

While Rifkin was unavailable for comment, insiders say
the meeting yesterday was as scary as some of the
director's movies. Sources report hearing a series of
screamed threats, including "Somebody will pay for this!", and several golf carts were later
discovered overturned. A billboard for Stab 3 was also defaced. While many would argue
the success of the Stab series is largely due to the talents of Mr. Rifkin, his tantrum at the
lot left many studio employees feeling puzzled and nervous. "I'm gonna watch my back for
the next couple days," said D-girl Helen Combs.

Mr. Bridger, lunching at the Ivy with superagent Beverly Choi, seemed thrilled by the
studio's decision: "When I was a kid, Fred's movies gave me nightmares all the time. It's an
honor to replace him."
