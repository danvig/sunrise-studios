unrise Studios has pulled the third and final knife
from the "Stab" drawer, according to industry
insiders. Sources say hot teen horror scribe Will
Kennison dropped off a script for the series' last installment
(predictably titled "Stab 3") on Monday AM and chief Don
Crosby was packaging the film by lunch.

The tight-lipped Crosby would only offer that
"Stab 3" is "going to be the best 'Stab' yet," and
"Stab" producer John Milton was unusually quiet:
"Will has outdone himself on the first draft and has
a great surprise ending fans will love. I plan to
keep it that way."

Tori Spelling has no comment yet on whether she will be returning to the role of Sidney,
but Fred Rifkin is rumored to be on board to direct the final chapter. Industry vultures are
already buzzing above the Sunrise lot, hoping to get their up-and-coming teen stars signed
up with the franchise. Casting begins officially in a few weeks. The "Stab" franchise is
based on the best selling books, "The Woodsboro Murders" and "The Windsor Murders",
by Gale Weathers. -Sara Maslin

elevision news personality Gale Weathers inked a deal last week to be the new host of "Total Entertainment." Ms. Weathers is best known for her relentless reporting and subsequent novels on the notorious Woodsboro murders as well as for her ability to take a punch. However, insiders say her stormy track record prevented the ambitious reporter from landing a coveted gig on an unidentified top-rated newsmag. Skies darkened last month when Sunrise Studios TV department left Weathers out in the cold and canceled her show "Top Story." Weathers, a self-proclaimed "survivor," declared the "Total" deal "mutually beneficial for the network, myself, and the world in general." In addition to the show, Weathers says its high tide for her own website, www.galeweathers.com. Fans of the "Weather" channel will have to wait a few more weeks, however. The site is scheduled to blow into e-town November 22nd. - Jason Voorjes

DUSTY BACKS DOWN

usty Penn ("Stab 2"), set to bow this weekend in "Gleason's: Life on the Ropes," has backed out of a deal to star in and produce the drama "Spin Doctor" for Vanguard Pictures. The dust has not yet settled and Penn's reasons for the sudden decision remain unclear.

The studio paid a high sum for the rights to the novel by Christopher B. Shields and was hoping for a marquee name to go with the on-the-run fable. Penn was that actor until today. "Obviously, we're very disappointed."
